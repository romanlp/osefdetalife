Technique 01

Provocation Technique

Deliberately absurd or counterintuitive prompts to jolt the mind out of habitual thinking. Each provocation forces a "why not?" response.

How it works: State something deliberately wrong, extreme, or impossible — then work backward to find the useful kernel. "SaaS billing based on outcomes" is a provocation against subscription norms. The breakthrough is finding which part of the provocation is actually desirable.

Technique 03

Retro-Future Slicing

An invented technique: imagine the 2035 version of a tool, then slice backward to find the smallest first step that contains its DNA.

How it works: Start with a bold future vision — "AI development orchestrator" — then ask: what's the thinnest slice of that future I can ship today? Each idea is a 2035 concept compressed into a 2026-viable prototype.

Synthesis & Themes

Where the four techniques converge — patterns that emerged across all 65 ideas.

🤖 AI as Co-pilot Cluster

The most persistent thread: AI doesn't replace the developer, it amplifies them. From pitch decks to code review to commit-to-blog, the pattern is always "human intent → AI execution."

🚀 Simple & Ship Cluster

Radical simplicity wins. The best ideas aren't the most ambitious — they're the ones you can build this afternoon. Constraints breed creativity, not limitation.

Idea 23 Idea 40 Idea 46 Idea 49–60

📊 Anti-Dashboard Insight

Dashboards that show what you haven't done, not what you have. Analytics that surface neglect, not vanity. The inverse metric is more powerful than the obvious one.

Idea 08 Idea 22 Idea 48

⚡ Friction as Feature

What if friction wasn't a bug? Notes that fade, tasks that question you, tools that show scarcity. Deliberate friction can create value that frictionless UX destroys.

💰 Micro-SaaS Economics

Outcome-based pricing, pay-per-use, parasitic infrastructure, revenue sharing. The subscription model isn't the only way — and for a solo dev, it might not be the best.
